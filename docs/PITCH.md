# Pitch

## 60-Second Script

Freelancers, agencies, and small vendors lose time and cash flow when invoices are late, disputed, or handled through platforms that custody funds. The problem gets sharper with AI agents: an agent can trigger a payment, but the buyer still needs proof of what was authorized, what was delivered, and why funds moved.

ArbiFlow is an Arbitrum-native agentic escrow desk. A buyer or agent creates an invoice, signs a mandate bound to the exact payment requirement, funds escrow, and then both sides move through delivery evidence, refund windows, split settlement, SLA bonds, and final receipt generation.

The contract does the enforcement: no admin withdrawal, scoped signed action permits, ERC-1271 smart-wallet signatures, service-bond accountability, pending payout protection for smart-account recipients, and receipt-bound feedback and validator attestations.

The agent panel does the guidance. It reads live contract state and tells the connected wallet exactly what can happen next: pay, release, refund, attach evidence, settle, submit feedback, or wait for timeout.

The result is not just payment. It is auditable agent commerce: intent, escrow, evidence, settlement, reputation, and a portable receipt, all on Arbitrum.

## Storyboard

1. Hook: "AI agents can pay, but who proves what they were allowed to buy?"
2. Show invoice creation and payment requirement hash.
3. Attach signed mandate and authorized payer lock.
4. Pay and show funds escrowed.
5. Add delivery evidence and service bond status.
6. Release or accept split settlement.
7. Show finalized receipt hash, feedback root, validation root, and Arbiscan link.

## Closing Line

ArbiFlow turns agent payments into enforceable settlement workflows: signed intent before funding, objective evidence during delivery, and verifiable receipts after settlement.
